# Introduction
Long story short, I had a discussion with an old friend who is finishing his PhD in mathematical topology. He mentioned one of his projects on optimizing autoencoders to preserve topological features in the latent spaceâ€”something highly relevant to his research. That conversation made me realize that I had never actually done a full autoencoder project myself, so I decided to give it a try.

This project explores an autoencoder trained on multiparameter weather data from Berlin, focusing on reconstruction and monitoring reconstruction error as a simple form of anomaly detection, such as identifying faulty or unusual sensor readings. It was a fun and practical way to dive into autoencoder applications while connecting to real-world data.
